# Step-by-Step Guide: Installing Drupal Commerce 3 Stack

## Modules included in setup script, detailing what it is and exactly why it is being installed.

## Core E-Commerce Modules
`commerce_product`

What it is: The engine that handles your catalog structure, items, and pricing variations (sizes, colors, etc.).

Why we need it: Without it, Drupal has no concept of an online "product" to display or sell.

`commerce_cart`

What it is: The software that adds the physical "Add to Cart" buttons, the shopping cart page, and the item counter blocks.

Why we need it: It allows customers to collect items and temporarily store them while they browse your site.

`commerce_checkout`

What it is: The multi-step form system (Shipping -> Billing -> Review -> Complete).

Why we need it: It guides the customer through a clean path to submit their delivery data and finalize their order.

`commerce_payment`

What it is: The central background engine that handles transaction logic and links the checkout flow to banks.

Why we need it: It provides the framework required to safely process currencies and trigger payment actions.

## Payment Gateways
`commerce_stripe`

What it is: The connector module linking your store directly to Stripe's APIs.

Why we need it: It securely securely securely processes standard credit/debit card transactions directly on your site without card details hitting your server.

`commerce_paypal`

What it is: The connector module linking your store to PayPal's API endpoints.

Why we need it: It gives users the option to log into their personal PayPal accounts or use digital wallet shortcuts to check out.

## Fulfillment & Inventory
`commerce_shipping`

What it is: The physical fulfillment logic that calculates shipping weights, dimensions, and packing rules.

Why we need it: It determines how much to charge the customer for postage based on what is in their cart.

`commerce_stock`

What it is: The inventory tracking system that manages warehouse item counts.

Why we need it: It automatically drops your inventory counts when someone buys an item and hides the "Add to Cart" button if an item sells out.

## Site Architecture & SEO Tools
`admin_toolbar`

What it is: An enhancement that replaces the basic admin layout with drop-down navigation links.

Why we need it: It saves time by letting your team hover over menus to reach settings in 1 click instead of 4.

`pathauto`

What it is: An automated URL generator.

Why we need it: It instantly rewrites messy database links into clean, readable links (like converting mysite.com/node/14 into mysite.com/products/blue-shirt).

`simple_sitemap`

What it is: An automated sitemap XML generator.

Why we need it: It feeds a clean, updated map of all your product links directly to Google so your items can be discovered in search results.

`search_api` & `facets`

What it is: An advanced product catalog indexing and filtering system.

Why we need it: Together, they build the product search filters (like picking a price range, size, or category checkboxes) so customers can easily sort through your catalog.

`commerce_wishlist`

What it is: A personal customer save list.

Why we need it: It lets shoppers bookmark products they like to save for a future purchase.

`metatag`

What it is: A hidden metadata and structured data injector.

Why we need it: It adds behind-the-scenes code to your pages so search engines display your product names, pricing, and availability cleanly directly inside search results.

# INSTALLATION

## Step 1: Download all packages via Composer

Run this single command to download Commerce 3, payment processors, fulfillment, and required tools:

```
ddev composer require drupal/commerce:^3.3 drupal/commerce_stripe drupal/commerce_paypal drupal/commerce_shipping drupal/commerce_stock drupal/admin_toolbar drupal/pathauto drupal/simple_sitemap drupal/search_api drupal/facets drupal/commerce_wishlist drupal/metatag
```

## Step 2: Enable the Core Commerce & Payments

Run this command to enable the checkout, shopping cart, catalog engines, Stripe, and PayPal:

```
ddev drush pm:enable commerce_product commerce_cart commerce_checkout commerce_payment commerce_stripe commerce_paypal -y
```
## Step 3: Enable the Shipping & Inventory Control
Run this command to enable physical shipping configuration and stock management tracking:
```
ddev drush pm:enable commerce_shipping commerce_stock -y
```
## Step 4: Enable the Site Architecture, SEO, & Search Filters
Run this command to turn on drop-down administration menus, automated clean URLs, sitemaps, advanced catalog filters, and metadata tracking:
```
ddev drush pm:enable admin_toolbar admin_toolbar_tools pathauto simple_sitemap search_api facets commerce_wishlist metatag -y
```
## Step 5: Verify Your Work and Open the Site
Check that your installations are active, then launch your site with an automatic, single-use login link:
```
ddev drush pm:list --status=enabled | grep commerce
ddev launch $(ddev drush uli)
```
